The 3 Little Pigs: A Poem

Aarush Mehrotra, Prasidh Sivakumar, and Shriya Parvathy, February 27, 2019
Click here to go back

There once were three little pigs.

In no time, they grew big.

A case to leave, their mom was pitching.

Three pigs parted, ambitions itching.

Off went three no-longer-little pigs.



It was a hot, sunny day.

The first pig found hay.

To an open field he rode,

To build a humble abode.

Meanwhile, the two brothers went another way.



The second pig remembered a forest of his childhood.

Reminiscing, he built a house of wood.

To an open field he rode,

To build a humble abode.

The last brother went away to make a home good.



The last brother found a pile of bricks,

And started of with a good cement mix.

So far nothing went wrong,

He built his humble abode good and strong,

As he had none to fix.



Next came the big bad guy.

He was definitely not shy.

His breath could summon the winds.

The three pigs he wanted skinned:

He wanted them to die.



The wolf found a house of hay-

The first pig better pray!

The wolf blew the house down;

The first pig ran out of town.

He went away.



Now two pigs hid in a house of wood.

They better hope their house stood.

The wolf blew the house down;

The two pigs ran out of town.

The third brother, protect them he would.



The wolf came again, standing tough.

“Once again,” he said, “I’ll huff and I’ll puff!”

But the third pig had a plan:

To boil a beer can!

The wolf bolted the ceiling and landed in hot stuff.



T’was the wolf’s end.

There were pigs houses to mend:

Houses to fix,

Perhaps this time with bricks

Or following the latest trend.
